https://www.winemag.com/buying-guide/torbreck-2001-steading-red-barossa-valley/	Torbreck 2001 The Steading Red (Barossa Valley)	89	This GSM is almost the same blend as the Juveniles (with “only a few parcels' difference,” says winemaker Dan Standish), but it spends 18 months in oak. Me, I narrowly prefer the Juveniles' freshness—The Steading's palate shows oak and cedar, and the heavier tannins that go with it. Nose is pretty closed. Very good, and perhaps one to revisit in a year or two.
